United Nations Myanmar Confidence Restoration Organisation

(2018– )

In accordance with the IFMR MUN Security Council of 28 January 2018 session

Background

The Humanitarian needs on the ground of Rohingya crisis, the immediate attention that the horrific conditions of people living in Rakhine region draws, makes it absolutely vital that all the member nations of UNSC ensure that the current refugee tragedy does not become a catastrophe escalating to a civil war.

To ensure right to self-determination, right to self-confidence, right to education, right to health, right to equality, and basic human rights, the UNSC and its member nation on 28th January 2018 formulated the UNMCRO – an Ad Hoc Committee to address the “Ethnic Cleansing” prevailing in Myanmar. Apart from its primary purpose to put a halt to the persecution of minority group, the secondary purpose of the committee is to ensure the reparation of the Rohingyas in the Rakhine state of Myanmar, to ensure the availability of basic humanitarian needs such as food rations, shelter, medicine, vaccination, etc.

Obligations

1. Member parties undertake to adopt legislative, administrative and other measures to ensure the full realization of all human rights and fundamental freedoms for all individuals within their jurisdiction without discrimination of any kind on the basis of region or religion. To this end States parties undertake:

a) To take all necessary measures to the maximum extent of the available resources to ensure the enjoyment of the economic, social and cultural rights in this convention, and where needed within the framework of international co-operation;

b) to take effective measures to review (governmental), national and local policies, and to amend, rescind or nullify any laws or regulations which have the effect or purpose of creating or perpetuating such discrimination wherever it exists;

c) To ensure that any person or class of persons whose rights or freedoms as recognized in this convention are violated shall have an effective and appropriate remedy whether the violation has been committed by persons or entities acting in an official or private capacity and in this regard to ensure; That any person claiming such remedy shall have his or her right thereto determined by competent judicial, administrative or legislative authorities or any other competent authority provided for by the legal system of the member nation. That the judicial, administrative and other structures in this regard are accessible in law, process and fact to any person with ethnicity seeking to make such a claim;

d)To embody the principles of equality (of opportunity) and non-discrimination on the ground of region/religion in their national constitutions or other appropriate legislation, if not yet incorporated therein, and to ensure, through law and other appropriate means, the practical realization of these rights;

e) To mainstream ethnicity issues into all economic and social development policies and programmes, including specific allocation of resources to satisfy obligations towards persons with ethnicity of Rohingyas;

f) To take all appropriate measures to eliminate discrimination on the ground of ethnicity by any person, or organization;

g) To refrain from engaging in any act or practice of discrimination against persons with ethnicity and to ensure that public bodies and institutions act in accordance with this convention;

h) To promote and where appropriate undertake the research, development, production, application and dissemination of new technologies in order to make available and affordable to persons with ethnicity goods, services, equipment and facilities acceptable to them and aimed at their fullest inclusion in society, [and as appropriate based on the principle of universal design];

i) To establish credible and effective structures to oversee implementation and monitoring; to ensure a barrier free society through the establishment of an effective enabling environment; to provide particular protection and support for persons with ethnicity who are vulnerable on account of situations such as conflict and natural disasters or because of their status as children, women and persons living with HIV/AIDS

j) To discourage customs and cultural practices that are inconsistent with this convention.

k) When planning, developing, implementing and evaluating policies and legislation to give effect to this convention, to take appropriate measures to ensure adequate consultations with, and involvement of, persons with ethnicity (Rohingyas), their families and representative organizations.

Note: The above listed obligations are binding to the primary member nations (Myanmar, ARSA (Arakan Rohingya Salvation Army), Bangladesh). For the secondary nation, the obligations are binding to the extent where their own Constitution are not challenged. In case of any conflict of interest, the Constitutional obligations of the secondary nation stands over and above any other obligations.

Mandate

The United Nations Security Council, stands united on the formation of the of United Nations Myanmar Confidence Restoration Organisation. After reaffirming the resolutions 1265 (1999) and 1296 (2000) concerning the protection of civilians in instances of armed oppression and it’s recurrences. The ad hoc committee inter alia will look into the ethnic cleansing of Rohingya Muslims in the Myanmar Union and will work to alleviate their situation in the following ways:

  1. Assuring the granting of citizenship by Myanmar.
  2. Making sure the right to self-determination is maintained as a juis cogens.
  3. Persecution of the Rohingya Muslims be stopped immediately;
  4. The procedure of naturalisation and acceptance of the Rohingya muslims as one the indigenous populace of Myanmar.

Hierarchy

  1. Headed by the High Commission of Refugees aided by the United Nations Sectariat, the Procedural and Emergency Meetings will be chaired on rotating chairmanship based on alphabetical order of members with observer nations or organisations being called upon as and when required.
  2. The working forces will constitute of three different offices, the first being the Bureaucratic and diplomatic office of UNMCRO, the humanitarian and investigative office of UNMCRO, the Security and Preventive Forces of UNMCRO.
  3. The numbers and the hierarchy of the three officers are to be decided in the first meeting under the discretion of the members organisations and countries.
